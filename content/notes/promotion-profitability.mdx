Promotions are addictive.

They work every time. Volume goes up, the dashboard looks healthy, the founder feels like they've found the answer. Then the month closes and the margin isn't there.

The problem isn't the discount. It's that nobody ran the number before the brief went out.

A 20% discount on a £60 AOV product at 45% gross margin needs 34% more orders than baseline just to match the profit you'd have made doing nothing. That's before returns uplift, marketing spend and the fulfilment cost on the incremental volume.

Most brands calculate revenue. Not that number.

There's a second problem that takes longer to show up. Promotions train behaviour. Run enough of them and you create a segment that only buys on discount. The repeat rate looks fine until you strip out the discount-triggered purchases. What's left is a cohort that won't pay full price.
