---
title: The Problem With Returns Rate As A Metric
date: "2026-04-10"
slug: the-problem-with-returns-rate
---

Most brands track returns rate as a percentage of orders. It's the wrong metric.

Returns rate tells you the volume of the problem. It tells you nothing about the cost. Two brands can have identical returns rates and wildly different financial outcomes depending on what happens to the product once it comes back.

The number that matters is cost per return — and within that, how that cost is distributed across three categories: the hard cost of processing, the margin leakage from the product outcome (full-price resell, discounted resell, write-off), and the delivery subsidy if you offer free returns.

A 20% returns rate on a product that resells at full price is a very different business problem to a 20% returns rate on a product that ends up written off 40% of the time.

The diagnostic question is not "what is our returns rate?" It's "what does a return actually cost us, and which product category is driving it?"
